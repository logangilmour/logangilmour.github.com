"Why traverse this broken drudge?"

I asked a windly scrim of air.

"We burn so much, and yet it crells

again, a scrinch of living dare.

"I know not knots," a felix drubbed,

"that devil only grundling grope.

We'll spindle on until they're gone,

But look! A scarpy antelope!"

It flounced and jounced and wheeled about

and gave a bleating, boisterous shout.

Then dead it lay, 'mongst apeish bods

like everything, for we were gods.
